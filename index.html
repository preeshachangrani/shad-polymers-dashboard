Market News

BEARISH
Brent breaks below $80 on Iran peace talks
Brent crude fell sharply to $76.68–$77.90/bbl after the US and Iran agreed to end hostilities and reopen the Strait of Hormuz, reversing an earlier spike to $82.30. Lower crude feeds through to lower naphtha/propylene feedstock costs over time.
Source: CNBC, TradingEconomics
NEUTRAL
Hormuz traffic normalizing but risk premium persists
Iran has not targeted shipping for two consecutive nights and traffic through the Strait has picked up, with Kuwait, UAE and Iran itself increasing exports. Analysts expect prices will not fully revert to pre-March levels given lingering geopolitical risk through the rest of 2026.
Source: CNBC, TradingKey
BEARISH
SE Asia PP/PE/PVC panic-buying has stopped
Converters in Southeast Asia who stockpiled resin during the March–April Middle East escalation are now sitting on comfortable inventories and buying only on a need-to basis, easing spot pricing pressure for PP, PE and PVC regionally.
Source: Market reports
NEUTRAL
China–SE Asia PP arbitrage window opens
As Southeast Asia PP prices soften while China values hold firmer, an arbitrage window has opened between the two regions — a dynamic worth watching for sourcing flexibility on PP Raffia/Homo/Copo grades.
Source: Market reports
BEARISH
Global freight rates rising on Middle East conflict knock-on
A new wave of freight rate increases is building across global trade lanes due to the Middle East conflict and related Southeast Asia port disruption, even though Lagos-bound FCL rates ($2,970–$3,850) held flat month-on-month.
Source: Sino-Shipping, Market reports
BULLISH
Naira stability supports import planning
The naira strengthened 0.82% over the past month (officially ~₦1,363/$ vs. a ₦1,395–1,405 parallel rate), aided by stronger FX inflows and improved market confidence — a modest tailwind for buyers financing dollar-denominated PFIs.
Source: TradingEconomics, CBN

Polymer & Terms Reference

PP (Polypropylene)
Raffia, Homo and Copo grades sourced mainly from China (CHN Energy, Jinneng/Baofeng). Used for woven bags, packaging film, injection molding. Currently $1,415–$1,520/MT CFR Apapa depending on grade and manufacturer.
PE (Polyethylene)
LDPE, LLDPE, MLLDPE, HDPE (Injection/Blow/Film/Pipe) cover the bulk of quoted grades. Used in film, blow-molded containers, pipe. Widest family range in current price sheet.
PVC (Polyvinyl Chloride)
